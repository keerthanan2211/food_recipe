Nihari Ghost

Process:

  1. Heat 1/4 cup of ghee or oil in a large pot or pressure cooker.
  2. Add 500g of mutton pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute.
  4. Add 2 sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of wheat flour (optional) and roast for a minute.
  6. Add 1 tablespoon of Nihari masala, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon of cumin powder, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  7. Add 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 2-3 hours (or pressure cook for 30-40 minutes) until the meat is tender and the flavors are well infused.
  9. In a separate small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil.
  10. Add 1 sliced onion and cook until it turns golden brown and caramelized.
  11. Add the caramelized onions to the Nihari and mix well.
  12. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, sliced ginger, and green chilies.
  13. Serve hot Nihari Ghost with naan or rice.
