Paneer Chilli

Process:

  1. Cut 200g of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) into cubes. Set aside.
  2. In a bowl, make a batter by mixing 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of cornflour, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper powder, and salt to taste. Add water as needed to make a smooth batter.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  4. Dip the paneer cubes in the prepared batter and deep fry until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  5. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil. Add 1 tablespoon of finely chopped garlic and sauté for a minute.
  6. Add 1 tablespoon of finely chopped ginger and sauté for another minute.
  7. Add 1 chopped onion, 1 chopped capsicum (bell pepper), and 1 chopped green chili. Sauté until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  8. Add 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of tomato ketchup, 1 teaspoon of vinegar, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili sauce, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  9. Add the fried paneer cubes to the pan and toss gently until they are coated with the sauce.
  10. Garnish with chopped spring onions and coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot Paneer Chilli as an appetizer or as a side dish with fried rice or noodles.
