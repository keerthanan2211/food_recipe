Dal Tadka

Process:

  1. Wash 1 cup of yellow lentils (moong dal or toor dal) and soak them in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked lentils along with 3 cups of water, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, and salt to taste. Cook for 4-5 whistles or until the lentils are soft and cooked.
  3. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Add 2 chopped tomatoes, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon red chili powder, and salt to taste. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the spices are well combined.
  7. Add the cooked lentils to the pan and mix well. If needed, adjust the consistency by adding water.
  8. In a small pan, heat 1 tablespoon ghee. Add 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds, and a pinch of asafoetida. Let them splutter.
  9. Add 1-2 dried red chilies and a handful of curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds.
  10. Pour the tempering over the prepared dal and mix well.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot Dal Tadka with rice or roti.
