Malabar Fish Biryani

Process:

  1. Wash and soak 2 cups of basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil. Add 1 sliced onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add 2 diced tomatoes, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of biryani masala, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  5. Add 500g of fish pieces (such as boneless fish fillets) and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Add 1 cup of coconut milk and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70-80% cooked. Drain the rice and set aside.
  8. In a large biryani pot or deep pan, layer half of the partially cooked rice at the bottom. Top it with the prepared fish masala.
  9. Add the remaining rice as the top layer and garnish with fried onions, chopped mint leaves, and coriander leaves.
  10. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook.
  11. Once done, gently mix the layers and serve hot Malabar Fish Biryani with raita or salad.
