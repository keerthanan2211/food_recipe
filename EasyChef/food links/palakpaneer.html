Palak Paneer

Process:

  1. Wash and blanch 2 cups of spinach leaves in boiling water for 2 minutes. Drain the water and immediately transfer the spinach to a bowl of ice water to retain the green color. Drain again and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee.
  3. Add 1 chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  5. Add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  6. Add the blanched spinach leaves and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down.
  8. Transfer the mixture to a blender and blend into a smooth paste.
  9. Return the blended spinach mixture to the pan.
  10. Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  11. Add 200 grams of paneer cubes (Indian cottage cheese) and cook for 5 minutes.
  12. Stir in 1/4 cup of cream or yogurt (optional) for a richer taste.
  13. Garnish with a drizzle of cream and serve hot Palak Paneer with rice, roti, or naan.
