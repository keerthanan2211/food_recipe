Pav Bhaji

Process:

  1. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter.
  2. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  5. Add 2 boiled and mashed potatoes, 1 cup of mixed vegetables (such as peas, carrots, cauliflower, and bell peppers), and salt to taste. Mix well.
  6. Add 2 tablespoons of Pav Bhaji masala and 1 teaspoon of red chili powder. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Mash the mixture using a potato masher until it reaches a thick and smooth consistency.
  8. In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of butter. Add a pinch of asafoetida, 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  9. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  10. Add 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  11. Add 1 chopped tomato, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  12. Add the prepared mashed Bhaji to the pan and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  13. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  14. For serving, heat the Pav (bread rolls) on a griddle with a little butter.
  15. Serve hot Pav Bhaji with the buttered Pav, chopped onions, lemon wedges, and a side of salad.
