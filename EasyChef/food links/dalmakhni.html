Dal Makhani

Process:

  1. Wash 1 cup of black lentils (urad dal) and 1/4 cup of kidney beans (rajma). Soak them in water overnight. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked lentils and kidney beans along with 4 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder. Cook for 6-7 whistles or until the lentils are soft and well-cooked.
  3. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or butter. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  7. Add 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  8. Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the pan and mix well. Cook for another 15-20 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  9. In a small pan, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee. Add 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  10. Add a pinch of asafoetida, 1 dried red chili, and a handful of chopped coriander leaves. Fry for a few seconds.
  11. Pour the tempering over the prepared dal makhani and mix well.
  12. Garnish with a dollop of butter and additional chopped coriander leaves.
  13. Serve hot Dal Makhani with naan, rice, or roti.
